Status System

Engineering documentation review statuses.

A controlled status vocabulary for tracking whether pages are drafted, reviewed, evidence-ready, approved, or blocked by missing engineering layers.

Status Rule

Status should describe engineering maturity, not writing progress alone.

Status Definitions

Use these statuses consistently across Technical Center categories, node pages, and execution subpages.

draft

Initial page exists. Structure or content may be incomplete.

architecture-reviewed

Taxonomy, hierarchy, routing, links, role paths, and related systems are checked.

engineering-reviewed

Execution logic, sequencing, node behavior, risk logic, and inspection workflow are checked.

evidence-reviewed

Evidence placeholders or real visuals are mapped to the required engineering proof type.

approved

Architecture, engineering depth, evidence readiness, and UI consistency are acceptable for publication.

needs-depth

Page is structurally present but lacks sufficient engineering explanation or execution workflow.

needs-evidence

Page requires real images, annotated diagrams, QC frames, project photos, or logistics visuals.

needs-risk-analysis

Page lacks failure modes, risk paths, warning logic, or inspection checkpoints.

Status Transition Logic

Review status should move through engineering maturity gates rather than one-step approval.

01 / Draft

Create page structure with required sections and source alignment.

02 / Architecture Review

Confirm taxonomy, internal links, role path, route, and related-system relationship.

03 / Engineering Review

Check execution logic, sequence, failure risks, node behavior, and inspection workflow.

04 / Evidence Review

Map evidence needs and verify diagrams, project photos, QC frames, or logistics visuals.

Approval Gate

A page becomes approved only after architecture-reviewed, engineering-reviewed, and evidence-reviewed are complete, with no open needs-depth, needs-evidence, or needs-risk-analysis flag.

Blocking Conditions

These conditions prevent approval even when the page looks visually complete.

Architecture Block

Page is orphaned, misclassified, weakly linked, or disconnected from related systems.

Engineering Block

Page lacks sequencing, node behavior, failure logic, or inspection checkpoints.

Evidence Block

Page depends on claims or diagrams but has no required real evidence plan.
