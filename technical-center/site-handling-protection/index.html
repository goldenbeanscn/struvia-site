Site Handling & Protection

Site handling and protection documentation.

Handling methodology covers multi-worker lifting, center-sag prevention, dry storage, pallet spacing, waterproof covering, cutting debris removal, rust prevention, rain control, and neutral cleaning.

Technical Overview

Site handling is part of engineering quality. Poor lifting, unsupported storage, rain exposure, surface scratches, cutting filings, and improper cleaning can affect facade appearance and installation performance.

01 / Lifting

Distributed support

Panels over 3 m should be supported at both ends and the center where practical.

02 / Storage

Dry and ventilated

Use flat, firm, elevated supports with spacing close enough to prevent sagging.

03 / Cutting

Filings control

Cut back-side-up where practical and remove metal debris immediately.

04 / Cleaning

Coating protection

Use water or neutral detergent; avoid strong acid, alkaline, or abrasive cleaning.

Practical Workflow

Site protection sequence

Protection begins at receiving and continues through storage, cutting, installation, cleaning, and handover.

01

Receive

Inspect packages, edges, coating, moisture, labels, and batch grouping.

02

Store

Keep panels dry, elevated, ventilated, and evenly supported.

03

Cut

Support panels, control filings, clean debris, and protect cut edges.

04

Clean

Remove stains and residue with coating-safe methods before final inspection.

Failure & Checkpoint Architecture

Engineering documentation should describe how workflows break, where risk concentrates, and what must be checked before the next step proceeds.

Moisture Caution

Water control depends on overlap, drainage exits, flashing, drying paths, and sequencing. Do not block intended exits.

Thermal Discontinuity Risk

Joints, fasteners, trims, openings, cuts, and damaged cores can interrupt insulation continuity.

Cutting Corrosion Alert

Metal filings must be removed from coated surfaces and cut edges before moisture exposure.

Unsupported Storage

Wide pallet spacing or uneven supports can deform long panels under their own weight.

Improper Overlap

Reverse-lap sequencing can direct water behind the cladding instead of outward.

Checkpoint Rule

Verify the control point before moving to the next stage: seat, fix, close, drain, clean, inspect.

FAQ

Why use three workers for long panels?

Distributed support prevents center sag, twisting, and localized deformation.

Can panels be stored outdoors?

Only with temporary waterproof covering, elevation, flat support, and ventilation awareness.

Why remove metal filings?

Filings can adhere to coatings or cuts and create corrosion risk if left in place.

What cleaners are appropriate?

Use water or neutral detergent; avoid strong acid-base cleaners and abrasive methods.
