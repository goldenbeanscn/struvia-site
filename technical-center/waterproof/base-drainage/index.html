Waterproof / Base Drainage

Base drainage execution reference.

Lower-edge drainage logic for starter trim, water exits, support conditions, debris control, and moisture trap prevention.

Execution Scope

Base node: starter datum, lower trim, water exit, blocked outlet risk, rainy-region inspection.

Engineering Overview

The base of the facade is the final release point for incidental water. If the lower trim traps water, the system can create hidden moisture conditions even when upper surfaces shed rain correctly.

Failure Risks

Blocked Outlet

Sealant, foam, debris, or trim placement blocks drainage.

Hidden Ponding

Water collects inside the starter or lower edge condition.

Wet Storage Transfer

Panels installed after wet storage introduce moisture at the lower edge.

Assembly Sequence

01 / Datum

Set base line and confirm substrate support.

02 / Starter Trim

Install lower receiving trim without reverse slope.

03 / Drainage Exit

Confirm water release holes or openings where required.

04 / Inspection

Check exits after panel seating, fixing, and cleaning.

Node Behavior

  • Water must move down and out.
  • Lower trim should not become a reservoir.
  • Drainage exits must remain clear after sealant, foam, or cleaning.
  • Rainy regions require stricter lower-edge inspection.

Inspection Checkpoints

Outlet Open

Drainage route is visible or confirmed by detail.

No Reverse Slope

Base trim does not direct water inward.

Debris Removed

Cuttings, dust, and packaging debris are cleared from lower trim.

Photo Record

Capture lower edge before final handover.

Common Failure Conditions

Over-Sealing

Installer seals the lower path that was intended to drain.

Starter Trap

Starter trim holds water behind the facade.

Uninspected Base

Lower detail is covered before water path is checked.
