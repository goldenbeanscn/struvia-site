Waterproof / Base Drainage

Base drainage execution reference.

Lower-edge drainage logic for starter trim, outlet continuity, support conditions, cleaning control, and moisture trap prevention.

Execution Scope

Starter datum, lower trim, water exits, debris control, rainy-region inspection, handover evidence.

Engineering Overview

The base of the facade is the final release point for incidental water. A correct upper water path still fails if the lower trim stores water, blocks drainage, or hides debris. The base condition must support panel alignment while allowing water to move down and out.

Control Layer

Lower trim and starter conditions should support the panel, protect the edge, and maintain an open drainage route.

Execution Hold Point

Confirm base line, trim slope, drainage outlet, and debris removal before repeated panel installation continues.

Evidence Required

Starter trim before panel seating, outlet close-up, cleaned lower edge, and final base condition.

Failure Risks

Base failures are commonly caused by well-intended over-sealing or incomplete cleaning.

Blocked Outlet

Sealant, foam, debris, or trim placement blocks the lower drainage path.

Risk path: drainage blocked -> hidden ponding -> wet lower edge.
Reverse Slope

Starter trim directs water inward or holds it against the panel edge.

Risk path: trim geometry -> water storage -> moisture exposure.
Wet Storage Transfer

Panels installed after rain exposure or improper storage introduce moisture at the base.

Risk path: wet panel -> closed base -> trapped moisture.

Assembly Sequence

01 / Establish Datum

Set base line, verify substrate continuity, and confirm support behind the starter location.

02 / Set Starter Trim

Install lower receiving trim without reverse slope or compression that closes drainage.

03 / Confirm Drainage Exit

Verify outlet holes, release openings, or designed gaps before panel seating conceals the route.

04 / Clean & Inspect

Remove filings, dust, packaging debris, and sealant overflow from the lower trim after installation.

Node Behavior

  • Water path must terminate outside the assembly.
  • Starter trim should not become a reservoir.
  • Cleaning is part of waterproof execution, not a cosmetic step.
  • Rainy or humid regions need stricter lower-edge inspection.

Inspection Checkpoints

Outlet Open

Drainage route is visible, documented, or confirmed by detail before handover.

No Reverse Slope

Base trim does not direct water inward or create a pocket.

Debris Removed

Metal filings, dust, packaging fragments, and sealant residue are cleared from the lower edge.

Panel Seating

Panel sits without crushing the lower drainage path.

Before Panel Run

Check starter alignment and outlet logic on the first bay.

After Cutting

Remove filings immediately to reduce staining or corrosion risk.

Drainage Warning

Do not close the lowest path with sealant unless the project detail provides another verified outlet.

Common Failure Conditions

Over-Sealing

The installer seals a release gap that was intended to drain.

Starter Trap

Lower trim holds water behind the panel edge.

Uninspected Base

The base is covered or repeated before the drainage path is checked.

Failure Chains & Verification Workflow

The base detail must be verified as a water-release node, not only as a straight starting line.

Blocked Drainage Chain

Sealant, foam, or debris closes the outlet -> water collects inside the starter trim -> lower panel edge remains wet -> reopen outlet and clean before handover.

Cutting Corrosion Chain

Field cutting leaves filings in lower trim -> filings become wet -> staining or corrosion risk develops -> remove debris immediately after cutting.

Risk Visualization

Mark the base trim as a moisture-trap zone, the outlet as a warning zone, and long lower runs as inspection bands.

Before Install

Confirm base datum, substrate support, starter trim orientation, and intended drainage exit.

During Install

Verify the panel does not crush the outlet and sealant does not close the release route.

After Install

Inspect outlet openness, lower trim cleanliness, and base-line continuity.

Prevention Workflow

Treat drainage gaps as control features. Clean filings before rain exposure and record the base outlet before handover.
