Waterproof / Window Flashing

Window flashing execution reference.

Control logic for head flashing, jamb trim, sill release, panel cut edges, and water path continuity around openings.

Execution Scope

Opening node: head, jamb, sill, trim sequence, drainage path, inspection record.

Engineering Overview

Window openings interrupt the panel field and create concentrated water-entry risk. Flashing must shed water outward before trims close the opening edges. Trims organize the edge; they do not replace project-specific flashing requirements.

Failure Risks

Head Leak

Water reaches the top of the opening and is not redirected outward.

Sill Trap

Sill trim collects water without a release path.

Trim Conflict

Jamb or finishing trim interrupts the flashing lap direction.

Assembly Sequence

01 / Opening Review

Confirm rough opening, reveal depth, substrate, cut lines, and panel direction.

02 / Head Path

Set head flashing to shed water over compatible lower elements.

03 / Jamb Closure

Install side trims without blocking head or sill water movement.

04 / Sill Release

Protect sill edge and confirm water can exit outward and downward.

Node Behavior

  • Head flashing is upstream protection.
  • Jamb trims close cut edges but must not reverse water path.
  • Sill must release incidental water.
  • Inspect before final sealant or closure hides the lap.

Inspection Checkpoints

Head Lap

Upper flashing sheds over the exterior face or compatible receiving layer.

Jamb Edge

No exposed core remains along side cuts.

Sill Outlet

Water release path is open after trim installation.

Final Record

Capture close-up and wide context before closure is concealed.

Common Failure Conditions

Reverse Lap

Trim or panel edge directs water behind flashing.

Blocked Sill

Sealant or foam closes the intended water exit.

Unprotected Cut

Cut edge remains exposed behind an opening trim.
