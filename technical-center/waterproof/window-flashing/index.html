Waterproof / Window Flashing

Window flashing execution reference.

Opening-node control for head flashing, jamb closure, sill release, panel cut edges, drainage continuity, and inspection before concealment.

Execution Scope

Head path, jamb trim, sill outlet, edge closure, water path audit, photo evidence.

Engineering Overview

Window openings interrupt the repeated panel field and concentrate water-entry risk at head, jamb, sill, and cut-edge transitions. The flashing sequence must move water outward before trim closure hides the lap direction. Trims protect and organize edges; they should not be treated as a substitute for project-specific flashing or opening waterproofing.

Control Layer

Water must be collected at the head, directed past the jambs, and released at the sill without entering the panel core or back side of the cladding.

Execution Hold Point

Inspect head lap, jamb edge protection, and sill outlet before final sealant, trim closure, or repeated installation.

Evidence Required

Head close-up, jamb close-up, sill drainage close-up, and wide opening context tied to the panel field.

Failure Risks

Opening failures often begin as small sequencing errors that become concealed by trim.

Head Leak

Water reaches the top of the opening and is not redirected outward over compatible lower layers.

Risk path: head gap -> hidden backflow -> wet opening edge.
Sill Trap

Sill trim or sealant creates a pocket where incidental water cannot exit.

Risk path: collected water -> blocked release -> moisture retention.
Trim Conflict

Jamb trim interrupts flashing direction or creates an upward-facing catch point.

Risk path: visual closure -> reversed lap -> concealed leakage.

Assembly Sequence

Use the sequence as a field checklist. Do not close the next layer until the active control point is visible and checked.

01 / Opening Review

Confirm rough opening, reveal depth, substrate condition, panel orientation, trim type, and cut-line location.

02 / Head Path

Set head flashing so water sheds outward and over lower compatible layers. Avoid upward-facing joints.

03 / Jamb Closure

Close side cuts while preserving the head-to-sill water path. Jamb trims should protect edges without blocking drainage.

04 / Sill Release

Protect the sill edge and confirm incidental water can exit outward and downward after trim placement.

Node Behavior

  • Head flashing is the upstream water-control element.
  • Jamb trims protect cut edges but must not reverse the lap.
  • Sill trim must release incidental water instead of storing it.
  • Final appearance is not evidence of correct water path.

Inspection Checkpoints

Inspection should occur before concealment and again after trim closure.

Head Lap

Upper flashing sheds over the exterior face or compatible receiving layer.

Jamb Edge

No exposed core or cut steel remains along side cuts.

Sill Outlet

Water release path remains open after sealant, trim installation, and cleaning.

Fastener Impact

Fasteners do not distort trim or create open water-entry points.

Before Closure

Photograph lap direction and edge protection while the control layer is still visible.

After Closure

Check for upward-facing gaps, blocked sill exits, loose trims, and unsealed cut exposure.

Moisture Warning

Do not seal a drainage outlet simply because it looks like a gap. Confirm the intended water path first.

Common Failure Conditions

Reverse Lap

Trim or panel edge directs water behind flashing rather than outward.

Blocked Sill

Sealant, foam, or debris closes the intended water exit.

Unprotected Cut

Panel core or cut steel remains exposed behind an opening trim.

Failure Chains & Verification Workflow

Use this section as the execution validation layer for opening details before the trim closes the inspection view.

Trapped Moisture Chain

Head gap or sill pocket -> water enters opening edge -> outlet is blocked -> moisture remains behind trim -> inspect head lap, jamb closure, and sill release before final closure.

Wrong Overlap Chain

Trim or flashing laps backward -> rain is routed behind the panel edge -> cut edge becomes wet -> reset lap direction before repeating the opening detail.

Risk Visualization

Mark the opening head as a warning zone, the sill as a moisture-trap zone, and jamb edges as trim-closure inspection bands.

Before Install

Confirm opening dimensions, substrate, flashing sequence, trim depth, and cut-edge protection plan.

During Install

Verify head shedding, jamb edge closure, and sill drainage before sealant or trim hides the control layer.

After Install

Check for upward-facing gaps, loose trims, blocked sill outlets, and exposed core.

Correction Workflow

Stop closure, reopen blocked paths, correct reverse laps, clean debris, re-photograph the detail, then continue.
