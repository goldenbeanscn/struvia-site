Installation / Interlock Sequence

Interlock sequence execution reference.

Panel-to-panel engagement logic for receiving edge preparation, seating, screw-line control, joint inspection, and repeatable facade rhythm.

Execution Scope

Clean edge, align panel, seat interlock, fix screw line, inspect joint, repeat bay.

Engineering Overview

Interlocking is an installation sequence, not a finished visual condition. The receiving edge must remain clean and undamaged, the next panel must fully engage, and fixing should only lock the panel after alignment is confirmed. Small seating errors can repeat across the facade.

Control Layer

The interlock controls alignment and edge coverage; the screw line controls restraint; the substrate controls load transfer.

Execution Hold Point

Confirm continuous seating before the panel is fixed. Once fastened, misalignment becomes harder to correct without damage.

Evidence Required

Receiving edge condition, panel seating frame, screw-line close-up, and final joint record.

Failure Risks

Partial Seating

Joint appears visually closed but is not fully engaged along the run.

Risk path: false closure -> weak engagement -> water or movement risk.
Premature Fixing

Screws lock a misaligned or unseated panel before correction.

Risk path: early screw -> forced correction -> face distortion.
Edge Damage

Dragged, bent, or debris-filled edges prevent continuous engagement.

Risk path: damaged edge -> interrupted joint -> repeated field error.

Assembly Sequence

Every panel run should follow the same rhythm so field crews can repeat the detail without improvising.

01 / Clean Receiving Edge

Remove dust, metal filings, packaging residue, and deformation from the interlock edge.

02 / Align Panel

Bring the next panel into position without dragging, twisting, or levering against the finished face.

03 / Seat Interlock

Confirm full engagement along the panel length before any final fixing.

04 / Fix & Inspect

Fasten through the correct screw zone and inspect joint gap, face plane, and repeated rhythm.

Node Behavior

  • Interlock engagement happens before restraint.
  • Fastening transfers load only when the substrate is suitable.
  • Over-fixing can distort the panel face or joint line.
  • Repeated bay inspection prevents accumulated error.

Inspection Checkpoints

Engagement

Joint is seated continuously, not only at one visible point.

Screw Zone

Fastener line hits suitable substrate and does not split, crush, or distort the panel.

Surface Plane

No local face distortion from over-fixing or forced alignment.

Repeat Rhythm

Panel run remains aligned across multiple bays and transitions.

Before Fixing

Run hand and sightline along the joint to confirm full engagement.

After Fixing

Recheck joint line and screw head condition before starting the next panel.

Sequencing Warning

Do not use screws to pull an unseated panel into place. Correct seating first.

Common Failure Conditions

False Closure

Visible gap looks acceptable but the interlock is not fully seated.

Over-Fixing

Screw pressure distorts panel face, joint profile, or trim relation.

Accumulated Error

Small rhythm error repeats until corners, openings, or transitions no longer align.

Failure Chains & Verification Workflow

Installation validation should stop sequence errors before they become repeated facade defects.

Unsupported Edge Chain

Receiving edge is damaged or unsupported -> panel seats unevenly -> fixing locks distortion -> joint opens or face plane waves -> correct edge and support before fixing.

Wrong Sequence Chain

Screws are installed before full seating -> panel is forced into place -> interlock or face distorts -> remove fixing pressure and reseat the joint.

Risk Visualization

Mark receiving edge, screw zone, and repeated joint line as warning zones; mark unsupported spans as deformation-risk bands.

Before Install

Check substrate, starter datum, panel edge condition, and screw-zone layout.

During Install

Confirm full seating along the run before fixing and verify screw spacing logic against the support line.

After Install

Inspect joint rhythm, face distortion, screw-head condition, and repeated bay alignment.

Correction Workflow

Stop the run, release forced fixing where needed, clean or correct the edge, reseat, refix, and record the corrected joint.
