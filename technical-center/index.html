Exterior Workflow Methodology

The technical center treats facade systems as workflow design. Materials, accessories, and envelope logic are evaluated by how they make exterior execution more predictable.

Methodological sequence

Exterior engineering knowledge should help teams understand what happens first, what controls alignment, what manages water, what protects transitions, and how quality is inspected before a wall area is repeated at scale.

01 / Plan

Map wall areas and transitions

Confirm substrate, openings, corners, accessory requirements, and panel sequence before installation starts.

02 / Align

Establish controlled baselines

Use starter trim, level control, and layout planning to prevent cumulative facade error.

03 / Fix

Mechanically secure the system

Coordinate screws, substrate conditions, panel seating, and concealed fixing relationships.

04 / Connect

Repeat panel and trim logic

Use interlocking relationships and connecting trims to reduce exposed joints and field improvisation.

05 / Close

Control edges and transitions

Use corners, finishing trims, and opening profiles to protect exposed edges and support facade continuity.

06 / Inspect

Verify before repetition

Check alignment, fixing stability, drainage paths, gaps, trim sequencing, and envelope continuity.
