Thermal / Continuity Joints

Thermal continuity joints execution reference.

Joint-level thermal logic for panel seating, PU core continuity, fastener bridges, trim effects, cut edges, and condensation risk.

Execution Scope

Thermal node: panel field, joint, fixing, trim, opening, substrate transition.

Engineering Overview

Panel-field insulation only performs as intended when joints, edges, openings, fasteners, and trims are coordinated. Thermal continuity must be inspected at every transition, not assumed from panel data alone.

Failure Risks

Open Joint

Gap interrupts insulation continuity and may permit air movement.

Metal Bridge

Fasteners and trims conduct heat around the insulated layer.

Condensation Zone

Cold surface plus air leakage can create moisture risk.

Assembly Sequence

01 / Map Thermal Layer

Identify where panel core should remain continuous.

02 / Seat Joint

Engage interlock or transition fully before fixing.

03 / Review Bridges

Check screw zones, trims, frames, and substrate transitions.

04 / Inspect Edge Cuts

Correct damaged core or unprotected cuts at joints and openings.

Node Behavior

  • PU core contributes panel-field insulation.
  • Joints and cut edges are continuity risk points.
  • Fasteners and trims can create conductive paths.
  • Whole-wall performance requires assembly review.

Inspection Checkpoints

Joint Seating

No visible or hidden gap at transition.

Core Condition

No damaged or missing insulation at cut zones.

Fastener Path

Metal penetration and substrate condition reviewed.

Opening Transition

Thermal layer continuity checked around windows and doors.

Common Failure Conditions

Panel Data Overclaim

R-value is treated as complete wall performance.

Cut Edge Gap

Insulation layer is interrupted at field cuts.

Unreviewed Bridge

Fastener or trim path bypasses insulation.
