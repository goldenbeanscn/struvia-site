Sequencing Gates

Each gate must be confirmed before the next step. Skipping gates is how small installation mistakes become facade failures.

Opening measured

Confirm head, jamb, sill, reveal depth, and panel cut lines.

Flashing path set

Head and sill flashing must shed water outward before trim closes the edge.

Jamb trim coordinated

Side trims do not interrupt head or sill drainage direction.

Final water path inspected

Check for reverse laps, blocked sill exits, and open cut edges.

Inspection System

Inspection should focus on the physical control point, not only the finished appearance.

Head condition

Look for exposed upward gaps above the opening.

Sill release

Confirm water can leave the sill zone.

Trim/flashing overlap

Flashing should direct water over compatible lower layers.

Cut edge closure

No exposed core should remain around the opening.

Failure Cause

Opening failures usually happen when trim is used as a visual cover without coordinating flashing direction and sill drainage.

Failure Analysis

Head Leak

Water reaches the opening head and is not shed outward.

Sill Trap

Sill detail collects water without a release path.

Trim-Flashing Conflict

Trim interrupts flashing direction or blocks drainage.

Warnings & Inspection

Flashing Logic Warning

Trims organize edges but do not replace project-specific flashing requirements.

Before Closure

Confirm alignment, overlap direction, exposed edge condition, and drainage or thermal continuity.

After Closure

Record photo evidence and inspect for gaps, blocked paths, loose trim, debris, or deformation.
