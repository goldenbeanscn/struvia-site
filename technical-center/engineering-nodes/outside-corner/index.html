Node Logic

  • External plane-change stability, impact protection, trim continuity, and visible corner alignment.
  • Sequence: Align first wall -> align return wall -> protect cut edges -> install outside corner trim -> inspect both faces.
  • Evidence should show the real detail condition, not a decorative facade view.
  • Inspection must happen before the detail is covered or repeated.

Sequencing Gates

Each gate must be confirmed before the next step. Skipping gates is how small installation mistakes become facade failures.

Corner line established

Set a visible vertical datum before closing either wall plane.

Edge protection planned

Both panel ends need trim coverage.

Overlap direction checked

Corner trim must support downward shedding on both faces.

Impact zone inspected

Check dents or deformation after handling.

Inspection System

Inspection should focus on the physical control point, not only the finished appearance.

Two-face alignment

Inspect both wall planes from multiple angles.

Open edge

No cut edge or core should remain exposed.

Trim restraint

Corner trim should not move or rattle under light pressure.

Water path

No upward-facing gap should direct water behind the corner.

Failure Cause

Outside corners fail when visual alignment is checked on one face while the return plane and water path are ignored.

Evidence Capture Protocol

Evidence should make the control point auditable after the work is covered, shipped, or repeated.

Failure Analysis

Impact Damage

Corner edge deforms during handling, installation, or service impact.

Open Cut Edge

Panel end remains exposed at a visible corner.

Reverse Water Path

Trim sequencing directs water into the corner instead of outward.

Warnings & Inspection

Overlap Caution

Outside corner trim must support downward water shedding on both wall planes.

Before Closure

Confirm alignment, overlap direction, exposed edge condition, and drainage or thermal continuity.

After Closure

Record photo evidence and inspect for gaps, blocked paths, loose trim, debris, or deformation.
