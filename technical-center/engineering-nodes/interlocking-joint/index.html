Engineering Node / Installation

Interlocking joint node.

Detail logic for panel-to-panel engagement, screw-line continuity, joint closure, and installation failure prevention.

Node Level

Overview -> workflow -> detail logic -> node systems -> evidence -> failure conditions.

Node Section

The interlocking joint is both an alignment detail and a continuity detail. It affects facade rhythm, water exposure, local stability, and inspection quality.

interlock receiving edge substrate panel field

Detail Logic

  • Seat the next panel fully before final screw fixing.
  • Keep joint engagement consistent along the full height or length.
  • Do not rely on interlock alone as structural fixing.
  • Check screw line, substrate hold, and trim closure together.
  • Inspect visible gaps before repeating the next panel bay.
Control Layer

Alignment, local stability, edge exposure reduction, and joint continuity.

Sequence

Starter datum -> first panel -> interlock seating -> screw fixing -> joint inspection.

Evidence

Close-up interlock photos, screw-line frames, panel seating screenshots, and final joint inspection images.

Failure Conditions

Partial Seating

Panel appears aligned from the face but the interlock is not fully engaged.

Premature Fixing

Screws lock a misaligned panel before the joint is seated.

Weak Substrate

Fasteners do not transfer load because substrate condition was not reviewed.

Warnings & Checkpoints

Before Fixing

Confirm panel seating, level, joint closure, and trim position.

After Fixing

Check screw stability, surface distortion, and visible gap continuity.

Improper Sequence Risk

Fixing before seating can create permanent joint gaps and water-sensitive discontinuities.
