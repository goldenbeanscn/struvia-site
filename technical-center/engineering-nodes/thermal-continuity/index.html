Engineering Node / Thermal Envelope

Thermal continuity node.

Detail logic for insulation continuity across panel fields, joints, trims, fasteners, cut edges, openings, and substrate transitions.

Node Level

Thermal layer -> bridge points -> air/vapor interaction -> condensation risk -> inspection.

Node Section

The PU core contributes insulation across the panel field. Continuity is weakened where metal, fixings, openings, gaps, or damaged cores interrupt the thermal path.

thermal control layerfastener bridgejoint / cut-edge review

Detail Logic

  • Maintain core continuity at panel joints where possible.
  • Review fasteners and metal trims as conductive paths.
  • Protect cut edges and damaged insulation areas.
  • Coordinate thermal logic with air leakage and vapor control.
  • Do not convert panel R-value into whole-wall performance without assembly review.
Control Layer

Thermal resistance, thermal bridge reduction, surface temperature consistency, and condensation risk control.

Sequence

Map panel field -> identify joints and fasteners -> review openings -> inspect cut edges -> verify assembly assumptions.

Evidence

Thermal continuity diagrams, joint photos, opening details, fastener layouts, and assembly calculation references.

Failure Conditions

Thermal Discontinuity

Gaps, damaged cores, or unplanned cuts interrupt insulation coverage.

Fastener Bridge

Metal fixings transfer heat through or around the insulated layer.

Condensation Zone

Cold surfaces plus moisture-laden air can create condensation risk.

Warnings & Checkpoints

Joint Check

Inspect panel seating and core continuity at repeated joints.

Opening Check

Review window, door, and penetration details for thermal breaks.

Thermal Discontinuity Risk

Do not claim full thermal envelope performance from panel field data alone.
