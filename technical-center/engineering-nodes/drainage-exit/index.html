Node Logic

  • Evidence should show the real detail condition, not a decorative facade view.

Sequencing Gates

Each gate must be confirmed before the next step. Skipping gates is how small installation mistakes become facade failures.

Entry possibility accepted

Assume incidental water may enter vulnerable details.

Exit designed

Lower edge, starter, or cavity must have a release route.

Exit protected

Opening should not invite debris blockage or pest entry where project rules apply.

Exit inspected

Confirm the outlet is still open after final closure.

Inspection System

Inspection should focus on the physical control point, not only the finished appearance.

Blocked outlet

Look for foam, sealant, dust, or trim blocking.

Ponding area

Check low points inside lower trims.

Rainy-region readiness

Confirm drainage strategy before handover.

Cleaning access

Exit should be inspectable or maintainable where possible.

Failure Cause

Drainage failures are usually created by over-sealing a detail that was supposed to release water.

Evidence Capture Protocol

Evidence should make the control point auditable after the work is covered, shipped, or repeated.

Close Detail

Sequence Frame

Capture the before, during, and after condition when possible.

Risk Frame

Record blocked paths, open edges, deformation, filings, or other failure evidence.

Failure Analysis

Blocked Outlet

Foam, sealant, dirt, or trim position blocks water release.

Hidden Ponding

Water collects inside the starter or lower trim.

No Inspection

Drainage exit is covered before the path is checked.

Warnings & Inspection

Moisture Warning

If water can enter, the system needs a visible or designed way for it to leave.

Before Closure

Confirm alignment, overlap direction, exposed edge condition, and drainage or thermal continuity.

After Closure

Record photo evidence and inspect for gaps, blocked paths, loose trim, debris, or deformation.
