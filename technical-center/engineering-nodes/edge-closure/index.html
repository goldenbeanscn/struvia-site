Inspection System

Inspection should focus on the physical control point, not only the finished appearance.

Exposed core

No visible foam/core should remain at the termination.

Scratch damage

Inspect coating near the cut edge.

Loose trim

Closure should be fixed and aligned.

Moisture exposure

Edge should not collect or hold water.

Failure Cause

Edge failures often begin as small unprotected cuts that become moisture, corrosion, or appearance problems later.

Failure Analysis

Exposed Core

Cut panel edge remains open to moisture, debris, or impact.

Loose Closure

Finishing trim does not restrain or cover the termination consistently.

Cutting Debris

Metal filings remain at the edge and increase corrosion risk.

Warnings & Inspection

Edge Protection Note

Every cut edge should be cleaned, protected, and inspected before handover.

Before Closure

Confirm alignment, overlap direction, exposed edge condition, and drainage or thermal continuity.

After Closure

Record photo evidence and inspect for gaps, blocked paths, loose trim, debris, or deformation.
