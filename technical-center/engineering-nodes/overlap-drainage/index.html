Engineering Node / Waterproofing

Overlap drainage node.

Detail logic for shingle-style water movement, drainage exits, lower trim behavior, and moisture trap prevention.

Node Level

Water path -> overlap direction -> lower exit -> flashing interface -> failure condition.

Node Section

Waterproof logic should move water downward and outward. If water can enter a condition, the detail must provide a controlled exit or drying path.

upper panel sheds over lowerstarter / lower trim

Detail Logic

  • Upper elements should shed over lower elements.
  • Lower trim must not trap incidental water.
  • Drainage holes or exits must remain open where specified.
  • Sealant must not block designed water release paths.
  • Opening flashing must shed water away from panel edges.
Control Layer

Bulk-water shedding, drainage, edge closure, and drying route continuity.

Sequence

Plan water-sensitive zones -> install receiving trims -> confirm overlap direction -> keep exits clear -> inspect lower edge.

Evidence

Water-path diagrams, starter trim close-ups, drainage exit photos, and rainy-region installation frames.

Failure Conditions

Reverse Lap

Lower component directs water behind the upper component.

Blocked Exit

Foam, sealant, debris, or trim placement prevents drainage.

Moisture Trap

Water enters a cavity or lower trim and cannot dry or drain.

Warnings & Checkpoints

Overlap Check

Confirm shingle direction before final fixing.

Drainage Check

Verify lower exits are visible, open, and not sealed shut.

Moisture Caution

Do not describe the panel as absolutely waterproof; performance depends on complete water-management detailing.
