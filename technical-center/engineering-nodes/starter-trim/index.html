Sequencing Gates

Each gate must be confirmed before the next step. Skipping gates is how small installation mistakes become facade failures.

Substrate line verified

Wall base is flat enough for the starter to act as a reliable datum.

Drainage route confirmed

Lower edge has a defined water release path before panels are seated.

Starter fixed and level

Fasteners hold the profile without twist or upward-facing gaps.

First panel seated

Panel is fully received before the next course is fixed.

Inspection System

Inspection should focus on the physical control point, not only the finished appearance.

Level tolerance

Check starter line continuously, not only at both ends.

Lower outlet

Confirm drainage holes or exit path remain open after fixing.

Panel engagement

Verify first panel is seated along the full run.

Debris control

Remove filings, dust, foam, and packaging debris from lower trim.

Failure Cause

Most starter failures come from treating the starter as a simple accessory instead of the control datum for the full facade run.

Evidence Capture Protocol

Evidence should make the control point auditable after the work is covered, shipped, or repeated.

Wide Context

Show where the node sits in the wall, package, opening, or workflow.

Close Detail

Show the joint, trim, edge, outlet, screw zone, or cut condition clearly.

Sequence Frame

Capture the before, during, and after condition when possible.

Risk Frame

Record blocked paths, open edges, deformation, filings, or other failure evidence.

Failure Analysis

Datum Error

A starter trim level error repeats across the full wall run.

Blocked Drainage

Lower trim traps water because drainage holes or exits are missing or sealed.

Poor First Seating

First panel is fixed before it fully seats into the receiving condition.

Warnings & Inspection

Drainage Exit Warning

Do not seal lower exits when the detail depends on drainage.

Before Closure

Confirm alignment, overlap direction, exposed edge condition, and drainage or thermal continuity.

After Closure

Record photo evidence and inspect for gaps, blocked paths, loose trim, debris, or deformation.
