Sequencing Gates

Each gate must be confirmed before the next step. Skipping gates is how small installation mistakes become facade failures.

Upstream side identified

Know which component receives water first.

Lower part installed first

Receiving trim or lower panel must support shingle direction.

Upper part sheds outward

Upper element overlaps and throws water over the lower layer.

Exit remains open

Drainage path is inspected before sealant or closure.

Inspection System

Inspection should focus on the physical control point, not only the finished appearance.

Reverse lap

Look for lower components tucked over upper components.

Upward gap

Horizontal gaps should not catch water.

Sealant blockage

Sealant must not close intended exits.

Lower staining risk

Review where water will leave the detail.

Failure Cause

Overlap failures happen when parts are installed in a visually neat order that reverses the actual water path.

Evidence Capture Protocol

Risk Frame

Failure Analysis

Reverse Lap

Water is directed behind the cladding by incorrect overlap order.

Upward Gap

Horizontal opening catches water instead of shedding it.

Blocked Path

Sealant or foam blocks an intended drainage path.

Warnings & Inspection

Improper Overlap Risk

Check overlap direction before final fixing or sealant work.

Before Closure

Confirm alignment, overlap direction, exposed edge condition, and drainage or thermal continuity.

After Closure

Record photo evidence and inspect for gaps, blocked paths, loose trim, debris, or deformation.
