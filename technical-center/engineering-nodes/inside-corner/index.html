Sequencing Gates

Each gate must be confirmed before the next step. Skipping gates is how small installation mistakes become facade failures.

Both planes checked

Confirm alignment and substrate condition on both wall faces.

Hidden edge protected

Cut edges are closed before they become inaccessible.

Drying path reviewed

Inside corner should not create a sealed wet pocket.

Corner closure inspected

Inspect before final trim conceals the joint.

Inspection System

Inspection should focus on the physical control point, not only the finished appearance.

Return-plane drift

Check that the second plane does not pull the corner out of line.

Moisture pocket

Review lower corner for trapped water risk.

Trim seating

Corner trim should cover both cut edges consistently.

Debris removal

Remove filings and dust before closure.

Failure Cause

Inside corners fail quietly because the risky edge is often hidden after closure.

Failure Analysis

Trapped Moisture

Water enters the inside corner and cannot drain or dry.

Hidden Open Edge

Cut edge or exposed core remains behind trim without inspection.

Sequencing Drift

Return wall is closed before corner alignment and water path are confirmed.

Warnings & Inspection

Moisture Warning

Inside corners need extra review because trapped water may be less visible after closure.

Before Closure

Confirm alignment, overlap direction, exposed edge condition, and drainage or thermal continuity.

After Closure

Record photo evidence and inspect for gaps, blocked paths, loose trim, debris, or deformation.
