Trim / Outside Corner

Outside corner execution reference.

Plane-change trim logic for edge protection, corner alignment, overlap continuity, water shedding, impact exposure, and two-face inspection.

Execution Scope

First plane, return plane, protected cut edges, corner trim, water path, impact check.

Engineering Overview

Outside corners combine visible alignment, exposed cut edges, water exposure, and higher impact risk. The corner should be treated as a two-plane node. Closing one face correctly is not enough if the return plane, trim cover, and water-shedding direction are not coordinated.

Control Layer

Corner trim protects two cut edges, holds the visual corner line, and prevents water from being directed into either wall plane.

Execution Hold Point

Inspect both planes, edge coverage, and trim lap direction before final fixing is repeated vertically.

Evidence Required

Pre-trim corner, trim placement, both-face alignment, and final corner close-up.

Failure Risks

Open Edge

Cut panel edge remains exposed at the corner or behind an incomplete trim cover.

Risk path: exposed cut -> moisture or corrosion exposure -> edge failure.
Plane Drift

Return wall misaligns even when the first face appears straight.

Risk path: one-face layout -> corner mismatch -> visible and functional conflict.
Reverse Closure

Trim edge catches water and routes it behind the panel.

Risk path: wrong lap direction -> concealed water path -> wet corner.

Assembly Sequence

01 / Establish Corner Line

Set vertical datum and check substrate condition on both wall planes.

02 / Close First Plane

Install the first panel field while maintaining edge allowance for the corner trim.

03 / Coordinate Return Plane

Align return-wall panel rhythm before the corner is permanently closed.

04 / Install Corner Trim

Cover both cut edges, maintain water-shedding direction, and inspect restraint.

Annotated Engineering Diagrams

Diagram intent: read the corner as a node where two wall planes, two cut edges, and one closure trim meet.

Node Behavior

  • Corner trim protects both exposed cut edges.
  • Both wall planes must remain aligned through the corner.
  • Water should shed around the corner, not into it.
  • Corner conditions need impact review because edges are more exposed than field panels.

Inspection Checkpoints

Two-Face Alignment

Check main face, return face, and vertical corner line together.

Edge Coverage

No exposed core or cut steel remains at either plane.

Trim Restraint

Trim is fixed without rattle, twist, or face distortion.

Water Path

No upward-facing gap or reverse lap at corner closure.

Before Trim

Record cut edges and plane alignment while both are still visible.

After Trim

Inspect restraint, edge coverage, and water-shedding direction from both faces.

Closure Warning

Do not inspect only the front elevation. The return plane is part of the same node.

Common Failure Conditions

One-Face Inspection

Only the main wall is checked, allowing return-plane drift.

Exposed Cut

Trim does not fully cover the panel edge on one or both planes.

Impact Deformation

Corner trim bends during handling or service and opens the closure line.

Failure Chains & Verification Workflow

Corner validation should check both planes, both cut edges, and the trim closure direction before the corner is repeated vertically.

Unsupported Edge Chain

Return edge lacks support or trim coverage -> corner line opens or distorts -> cut edge becomes exposed -> correct support and closure before final fixing.

Wrong Overlap Chain

Corner trim catches water or reverses the lap -> water is routed into the plane change -> moisture reaches the cut edge -> reset trim direction and recheck both faces.

Risk Visualization

Mark the corner edge as an impact warning zone, exposed cuts as moisture-risk bands, and the return face as a second-plane inspection zone.

Before Install

Confirm substrate support, two-face alignment, trim allowance, and cut-edge protection plan.

During Install

Check trim continuity, edge coverage, and water-shedding direction before fixing the full corner run.

After Install

Inspect return-plane drift, trim looseness, open cuts, impact deformation, and upward-facing gaps.

Correction Workflow

Stop the corner run, correct plane alignment or edge coverage, refix trim without distortion, and capture both-face evidence.
