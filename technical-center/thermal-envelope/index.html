Thermal Envelope

Thermal envelope documentation.

The insulated panel contributes to heat-transfer control, but practical performance depends on joints, trims, fasteners, openings, substrate transitions, vapor behavior, and assembly design.

Primary Sources

thermal-envelope-logic.md, thermal-performance-logic.md, performance-system.md

Technical Overview

Thermal-envelope logic reviews how exterior finish, PU core, backing, joints, trims, cavity design, and air/vapor control interact. R-value and U-value references should not be used as whole-wall guarantees without assembly review.

01 / Insulation

Panel field contribution

PU foam reduces conductive heat flow across the main panel body.

02 / Continuity

Joints and transitions

Thermal weak points occur at fixings, frames, corners, openings, and cuts.

03 / Moisture

Condensation risk

Thermal bridges and air leakage can create cold surfaces and moisture problems.

04 / Assembly

Whole-wall review

Complete performance depends on all layers, not the insulated panel alone.

Failure & Checkpoint Architecture

Engineering documentation should describe how workflows break, where risk concentrates, and what must be checked before the next step proceeds.

Moisture Caution

Water control depends on overlap, drainage exits, flashing, drying paths, and sequencing. Do not block intended exits.

Thermal Discontinuity Risk

Joints, fasteners, trims, openings, cuts, and damaged cores can interrupt insulation continuity.

Cutting Corrosion Alert

Metal filings must be removed from coated surfaces and cut edges before moisture exposure.

Unsupported Storage

Wide pallet spacing or uneven supports can deform long panels under their own weight.

Improper Overlap

Reverse-lap sequencing can direct water behind the cladding instead of outward.

Checkpoint Rule

Verify the control point before moving to the next stage: seat, fix, close, drain, clean, inspect.

FAQ

Can R-value describe the full wall?

No. It is a reference value; whole-wall performance requires assembly calculation.

Can thermal bridges be eliminated?

No universal claim should be made. Bridges can be reduced through detail coordination.

Does foil backing solve vapor control?

No. Vapor behavior depends on continuity, placement, climate, and wall buildup.

What needs inspection?

Joints, corners, openings, fasteners, trims, damaged cores, and penetrations.
