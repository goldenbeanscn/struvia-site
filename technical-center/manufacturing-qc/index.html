Technical Overview

The factory system is not only production capacity. It should be understood as repeatable exterior-system preparation: controlled materials, panel geometry, surface finish, bonding, packing, labeling, and QC checkpoints.

Failure & Checkpoint Architecture

Engineering documentation should describe how workflows break, where risk concentrates, and what must be checked before the next step proceeds.

Moisture Caution

Water control depends on overlap, drainage exits, flashing, drying paths, and sequencing. Do not block intended exits.

Thermal Discontinuity Risk

Joints, fasteners, trims, openings, cuts, and damaged cores can interrupt insulation continuity.

Cutting Corrosion Alert

Metal filings must be removed from coated surfaces and cut edges before moisture exposure.

Unsupported Storage

Wide pallet spacing or uneven supports can deform long panels under their own weight.

Improper Overlap

Reverse-lap sequencing can direct water behind the cladding instead of outward.

Checkpoint Rule

Verify the control point before moving to the next stage: seat, fix, close, drain, clean, inspect.

FAQ

Is factory content only about capacity?

No. The technical focus is consistency, inspection, and system readiness.

Why does packaging belong to QC?

Export damage can destroy installability even when production quality is correct.

Why track batches?

Batch control helps manage color consistency and project wall-area planning.

What is the QC endpoint?

A panel that remains installable after packaging, loading, storage, and handling.
