# Storage Protection System

This document defines storage protection methodology for Struvia exterior panels.

Reference base:

* uploaded handling, storage, cutting, and construction precautions document
* `knowledge/export-workflow.md`
* `knowledge/loading-logic.md`

Important interpretation:

* Storage is part of exterior panel performance protection.
* Poor storage can deform panels, damage coating, expose edges to water, and reduce installation quality.
* Do not describe panels as unaffected by weather or site abuse.

---

# Storage Philosophy

Storage should preserve:

* panel flatness
* coating condition
* edge integrity
* dry core and cut-edge condition
* package stability
* batch traceability

Core principle:

* panels should be kept dry, ventilated, elevated, and evenly supported.

---

# Dry Storage

Preferred method:

* store panels in a closed, dry, ventilated warehouse
* use a flat and firm storage surface
* keep panels away from standing water
* avoid long-term open-air exposure

Outdoor temporary storage:

* use waterproof covering
* raise panels with square timber or pallets
* prevent water from collecting under the stack
* keep ventilation where possible so covering does not trap moisture

---

# Pallet & Support Spacing

Support spacing affects gravity deformation.

Storage logic:

* place panels on pallets where possible
* use square timber support pads when pallets are not available
* keep timber or support spacing close enough to prevent sagging
* align supports so load is distributed along the panel length
* avoid uneven ground that creates point loads

Risk:

* wide support spacing can allow panels to deform under their own weight

---

# Waterproof Covering & Ventilation

Water protection requires both covering and drying awareness.

Controls:

* cover outdoor stacks with waterproof awning or sheeting
* avoid direct rain and soaking
* keep panels elevated
* prevent water from entering exposed edges or interiors
* maintain ventilation to reduce trapped moisture risk

Storage caution:

* covering should protect from rain without creating a sealed wet pocket around the panels

---

# Improper Stacking Risks

Common risks:

* unsupported storage
* uneven stacking
* excessive support spacing
* direct contact with wet ground
* rain exposure
* pressure on interlocking edges
* mixed-batch use without visual planning

Controls:

* use stable pallets or timber supports
* keep stacks level
* separate or protect vulnerable edges
* inspect panels before moving them to the wall
* keep batch groups organized for wall-area installation
