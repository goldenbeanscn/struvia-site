# Inspection System

The inspection system defines how Technical Center pages verify exterior panel execution before work is repeated, covered, shipped, or handed over.

Inspection is not a visual approval of the finished surface. It checks the control point that prevents moisture entry, deformation, thermal discontinuity, corrosion, or installation failure.

## Inspection Stages

### Before Install

- Confirm substrate condition and support continuity.
- Confirm panel orientation, trim type, cut lines, and installation sequence.
- Check storage condition, surface protection, and moisture exposure.
- Confirm required accessories, flashing, drainage outlets, and fasteners are available.

### During Install

- Confirm interlock seating before screw fixing.
- Check screw zone, spacing logic, and substrate engagement.
- Verify overlap direction before trim closure.
- Keep drainage paths open during panel seating and sealant work.
- Remove metal filings and cutting debris immediately.

### After Install

- Inspect visible gaps, reverse laps, loose trims, blocked outlets, face distortion, and exposed cut edges.
- Record close-up evidence before details are concealed.
- Capture wide context images showing relationship to wall field, openings, corners, and base conditions.
- Confirm correction work is completed before repeating the detail.

## Core Inspection Types

### Overlap Verification

Check that upper elements shed over lower elements and do not reverse the water path.

### Screw Spacing Checks

Verify the screw line reaches suitable substrate, does not distort the panel face, and follows the project fixing logic.

### Trim Continuity Checks

Confirm trims protect cut edges, maintain closure, and do not block drainage or interrupt flashing.

### Drainage Path Inspection

Confirm water can move down and out through the intended lower outlet, release gap, or drainage path.

### Flashing Closure Inspection

Confirm head, jamb, and sill conditions are coordinated before trim closure hides the lap direction.

## Inspection Evidence

Each inspection should produce:

- Wide context frame.
- Close control-point frame.
- Sequence frame where possible.
- Risk frame if a failure condition is found.
- Correction frame after rework.

## Approval Logic

A detail should not be approved only because it looks complete. It should be approved when the control layer, sequence, risk condition, and evidence record are all acceptable.
