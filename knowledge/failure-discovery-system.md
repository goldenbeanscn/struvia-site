# Failure Discovery System

The failure discovery system helps users start from a problem and find the right engineering reference.

## Failure Query Pattern

Each failure query should resolve to:

1. Failure definition.
2. Likely physical causes.
3. Inspection locations.
4. Related execution page.
5. Related node page.
6. Evidence needed.
7. Correction or prevention workflow.

## Failure Routes

### Trapped Moisture

Definition: water enters a detail and cannot exit or dry.

Likely causes:

- Blocked drainage.
- Sealed outlet.
- Sill trap.
- Reverse lap.
- Wet storage condition.

Inspection locations:

- Base trim.
- Sill outlet.
- Starter trim.
- Window opening.
- Lower panel edge.

Primary pages:

- `technical-center/waterproof/base-drainage/`
- `technical-center/waterproof/window-flashing/`
- `knowledge/failure-architecture.md`

Evidence needed:

- Drainage outlet close-up.
- Sill release image.
- After-cleaning lower trim frame.

## Thermal Bridge

Definition: metal or assembly transition bypasses the intended insulation layer.

Likely causes:

- Fastener path.
- Trim bridge.
- Open joint.
- Cut-edge gap.
- Opening transition.

Inspection locations:

- Joints.
- Fasteners.
- Trims.
- Window or door openings.
- Substrate contacts.

Primary pages:

- `technical-center/thermal/continuity-joints/`
- `technical-center/thermal-envelope/`
- `knowledge/thermal-performance-logic.md`

Evidence needed:

- Joint close-up.
- Cut-edge core photo.
- Fastener layout frame.

## Blocked Drainage

Definition: intended water exit is closed by sealant, foam, debris, or trim geometry.

Likely causes:

- Over-sealing.
- Cutting debris.
- Starter trim pocket.
- Poor cleaning.
- Uninspected base closure.

Inspection locations:

- Base drainage outlet.
- Starter trim.
- Sill release.
- Drainage exit node.

Primary pages:

- `technical-center/waterproof/base-drainage/`
- `technical-center/engineering-nodes/drainage-exit/`
- `knowledge/inspection-system.md`

Evidence needed:

- Outlet before closure.
- Outlet after panel seating.
- Cleaning completion frame.

## Corrosion

Definition: metal surface or cut edge is exposed to corrosion risk due to debris, moisture, or damaged coating.

Likely causes:

- Cutting filings left on surface.
- Wet storage.
- Unprotected cut edge.
- Debris trapped in lower trim.

Inspection locations:

- Cut edges.
- Lower trims.
- Opening cuts.
- Corners.
- Surface after cutting.

Primary pages:

- `technical-center/waterproof/base-drainage/`
- `technical-center/engineering-nodes/cutting-corrosion/`
- `knowledge/construction-protection-logic.md`

Evidence needed:

- Cut zone after cleaning.
- Lower trim after debris removal.
- Protected edge close-up.

## Unsupported Edge

Definition: panel edge, corner, base, or joint lacks support or restraint.

Likely causes:

- Weak substrate.
- Incorrect screw line.
- Trim not covering edge.
- Forced alignment.
- Handling deformation.

Inspection locations:

- Outside corner.
- Interlock joint.
- Base starter.
- Screw zone.
- Panel transition.

Primary pages:

- `technical-center/installation/interlock-sequence/`
- `technical-center/trim/outside-corner/`
- `knowledge/sequencing-checkpoints.md`

Evidence needed:

- Screw zone frame.
- Corner before trim.
- Joint after fixing.
