# Project Risk Prevention

Project risk prevention translates known failure chains into project-specific control steps.

## Project Risk Method

For each project page, document:

1. Project context.
2. Climate and application pressure.
3. System selection reason.
4. Installation workflow.
5. Risk conditions.
6. Inspection checkpoints.
7. Evidence required.
8. Correction and prevention workflow.

## Common Project Risk Controls

### Trapped Moisture

Prevention:

- Plan overlap direction.
- Keep drainage paths open.
- Avoid sealing intended outlets.
- Inspect sill and base release paths.
- Capture before-closure evidence.

### Wrong Overlap

Prevention:

- Verify shingle direction before fixing.
- Check trim and flashing sequence.
- Inspect corners, windows, and lower terminations.

### Blocked Drainage

Prevention:

- Keep outlets visible until inspection.
- Remove dust, filings, foam, sealant overflow, and packaging debris.
- Confirm drainage after panel seating.

### Thermal Bridge

Prevention:

- Distinguish panel-field insulation from whole-wall behavior.
- Review fasteners, trims, openings, and substrate transitions.
- Inspect cut-edge core condition.

### Unsupported Edge

Prevention:

- Confirm substrate and support line before installation.
- Check corner and base support.
- Avoid forcing panels into alignment with screws.

### Cutting Corrosion

Prevention:

- Cut carefully.
- Remove filings immediately.
- Clean lower trims and coated surfaces.
- Avoid rain exposure before cleaning.

## Project Evidence Acceptance

Project evidence should not be approved unless it includes:

- At least one installation sequence frame.
- At least one detail or trim close-up.
- At least one risk-prevention note.
- At least one inspection checkpoint.
- At least one related Technical Center system link.
