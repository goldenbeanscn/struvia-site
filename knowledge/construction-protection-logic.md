# Construction Protection Logic

This document defines cutting, construction, cleaning, and onsite protection logic for Struvia exterior panels.

Reference base:

* uploaded handling, storage, cutting, and construction precautions document
* `knowledge/installation-system.md`
* `knowledge/waterproof-logic.md`
* `knowledge/material-properties.md`

Important interpretation:

* Construction protection is part of facade quality control.
* Cutting debris, rain exposure, scratches, and improper cleaning can damage panel surfaces or edges.
* Do not exaggerate coating durability or corrosion resistance.

---

# Cutting Workflow

Cutting should protect the coating and cut edges.

Recommended logic:

1. plan cut location before cutting
2. support the panel so it does not vibrate or sag
3. cut with the back side facing up where practical
4. keep metal filings away from visible coated surfaces
5. remove filings promptly after cutting
6. clean with a soft brush, air cleaning method, or other non-damaging process
7. wear protective gloves during cleaning and handling

Engineering reason:

* metal filings can adhere to the coating or cut edge and create corrosion risk if left in place

---

# Rust Prevention Logic

Rust prevention during construction depends on debris and moisture control.

Risk sources:

* cutting filings left on the surface
* wet cutting debris
* exposed cut edges
* rain entering panel interiors
* construction residue left after installation

Controls:

* remove filings immediately
* keep panels dry during cutting and installation
* avoid construction during rain where water can contact panel interiors
* inspect cut zones before fixing panels permanently
* clean residue before handover

---

# Surface Protection

Panel surfaces should be protected during construction.

Controls:

* avoid scratching the coated face
* prevent impact from tools and other materials
* do not use panel surfaces as work benches
* avoid dragging panels across abrasive surfaces
* protect corners and interlocking edges during staging

Common damage modes:

* surface scratches
* impact dents
* coating contamination
* edge deformation
* visible color inconsistency from poor batch planning

---

# Rain Exposure Control

Construction should avoid uncontrolled water exposure.

Controls:

* avoid installation work during rain where practical
* prevent the inside of panels from contacting water
* keep stored panels covered and elevated
* protect cut edges and exposed interiors
* inspect wet panels before installation continues

Engineering caution:

* moisture exposure can increase corrosion and rust risk, especially around cuts, debris, and exposed edges

---

# Cleaning & Maintenance Logic

Cleaning should protect the coating.

Recommended cleaning:

* use clean water for ordinary construction stains
* use neutral detergent where needed
* remove construction residue promptly
* use soft cleaning methods that do not scratch the coating

Avoid:

* strong acid cleaners
* strong alkaline cleaners
* aggressive solvents
* abrasive cleaning that damages topcoat

---

# Construction Sequencing Protection

Protection should be built into the site sequence.

Sequence checkpoints:

* receiving inspection
* dry storage
* distributed lifting
* supported cutting
* filings removal
* panel installation
* rain and moisture check
* surface cleaning
* final visual inspection
