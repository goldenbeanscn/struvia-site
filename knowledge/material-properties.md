# Material Properties

This document defines material-property logic for Struvia exterior systems.

Reference base:

* uploaded technical specification Excel file

Important interpretation:

* Material properties explain behavior; they do not replace project engineering.
* Values should be used with test-method awareness and assembly context.

---

# Composite Material Structure

Spreadsheet structure reference:

* steel sheet
* aluminum foil backing or coated fabric
* polyurethane foam

Engineering interpretation:

* the system is a composite panel, not a single-material sheet
* each layer performs a different role
* performance depends on the relationship between layers
* layer continuity and bond quality are part of material behavior

Integrated layer logic:

* exterior steel layer protects and stabilizes the exposed face
* PU foam core provides insulation volume and lightweight body
* rear backing layer protects the core and influences rear-side moisture or vapor behavior
* bonding connects the layers into a single panel body

---

# PU Density Behavior

Spreadsheet density references:

* 40 plus or minus 2 kg/m3 in the Chinese sheet
* 40-45 kg/m3 in the English sheet

Engineering interpretation:

* density supports consistency of foam structure
* density influences weight, insulation body, handling, and composite feel
* density alone does not prove compressive strength, fire performance, water resistance, or long-term durability
* density should be evaluated with bond strength, core integrity, dimensional stability, and thermal data

Buildability relationship:

* controlled density supports predictable panel weight
* predictable panel weight supports handling and installation planning
* consistent core behavior supports repeatable facade execution

---

# Steel Layer Function

The exterior steel layer is the weather-facing and appearance-facing skin.

Functions:

* provides architectural surface
* protects the insulation core from direct exposure
* contributes panel stiffness
* supports dimensional stability
* receives coatings and finishes

Engineering caution:

* steel is more thermally conductive than PU foam
* edges and penetrations should be protected
* corrosion resistance depends on coating, environment, cut-edge treatment, detailing, and maintenance

---

# Backing Layer Function

The backing layer protects the rear side of the composite panel.

Functions:

* separates the PU core from rear-side exposure
* supports panel integrity during handling and installation
* may influence vapor or moisture behavior depending on wall assembly
* supports integrated panel manufacturing logic

Engineering caution:

* backing is not a complete waterproofing system by itself
* vapor behavior depends on continuity, placement, wall buildup, and climate
* rear-side cavities still require drainage and drying logic when designed

---

# Bond Strength Logic

Spreadsheet bond strength reference:

* 0.18 MPa

Engineering interpretation:

* bond strength describes the relationship between layers
* it supports composite action between facing, core, and backing
* poor bonding can weaken panel integrity, handling behavior, and long-term stability
* bond strength is not the same as fastener capacity, wind-load approval, or structural span capacity

Practical relevance:

* bonded layers help the panel behave as one integrated unit
* installation should avoid damage that separates layers
* cut edges and penetrations should be handled carefully to preserve composite continuity
