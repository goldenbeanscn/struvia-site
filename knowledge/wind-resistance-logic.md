# Facade Stability Logic

Wind acts on the exterior wall as pressure and suction.

Facade stability depends on:

* panel rigidity
* edge engagement
* interlocking continuity
* screw fixing
* trim restraint
* substrate strength
* spacing and layout
* corner and edge-zone detailing

Panel field behavior:

* the metal skin receives wind pressure across the exposed surface
* the composite panel body distributes pressure across the panel width and length
* the core and bonded layers help the panel behave as an integrated unit
* fixings and supports transfer load into the building structure

---

# Interlocking Support Logic

Interlocking helps the facade act as a continuous field.

Support functions:

* aligns adjacent panels
* reduces free exposed edges
* helps maintain plane continuity
* supports repeated installation rhythm
* reduces localized movement when properly seated and fixed

Limitations:

* interlocking is not a replacement for mechanical fixing
* edge engagement must be fully seated before screw fixing
* trims and corners must not be left as unsupported wind-sensitive edges
* long panels require handling and fixing discipline to prevent bowing or misalignment

---

# Installation Continuity Under Wind Load

Wind resistance depends on installation continuity.

Critical inspection points:

* starter alignment
* panel seating
* screw type and spacing
* substrate attachment
* vertical and horizontal joint closure
* corner trim restraint
* opening perimeter detailing
* lower and upper termination support

Failure-risk conditions:

* missing or loose screws
* misaligned interlock
* unsupported cut edges
* weak substrate
* excessive panel movement
* open joints at corners or openings
* wrong fixing pattern for building height or wind zone

---

# Project Review Logic

Wind design should consider:

* local design wind speed
* building height
* terrain and exposure
* facade zone pressures
* corner suction zones
* substrate type
* fastener pull-out and pull-over behavior
* panel span and support spacing
* code and engineer requirements

Engineering principle:

* wind pressure must have a continuous load path from panel surface to panel body, from panel body to fixings, from fixings to substrate, and from substrate to the primary structure
