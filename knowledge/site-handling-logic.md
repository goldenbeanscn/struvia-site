# Site Handling Logic

This document defines site handling methodology for Struvia exterior panels.

Reference base:

* uploaded handling, storage, cutting, and construction precautions document
* `knowledge/installation-system.md`
* `knowledge/loading-logic.md`
* `knowledge/lightweight-construction.md`

Important interpretation:

* This is a site-management framework, not a simple instruction sheet.
* Handling quality affects panel geometry, coating condition, edge integrity, and installation continuity.
* Do not treat lightweight panels as damage-proof.

---

# Panel Handling Philosophy

Panel handling should protect:

* panel straightness
* visible coating surface
* interlocking edges
* corners and ends
* cut edges
* installation fit

Core principle:

* long panels require distributed support because unsupported spans can sag or deform under their own weight

---

# Multi-Worker Lifting

Handling reference:

* for panels longer than 3 m, use at least three workers where practical
* position workers at both ends and near the center
* coordinate lifting and walking rhythm
* keep the panel level during movement

Why it matters:

* end-only lifting can allow the center to sink
* uneven worker movement can twist the panel
* concentrated support can create local deformation or surface grooves

Safe lifting workflow:

1. check panel length and route before lifting
2. assign end and center support positions
3. lift together on a clear signal
4. keep the panel level and supported
5. avoid dragging, twisting, and point loading
6. place panels onto prepared supports

---

# Distributed Load Handling

Distributed handling reduces deformation risk.

Control methods:

* support long spans at several points
* avoid carrying panels by only one edge
* use slings, lifting rods, or suitable tools where needed
* keep coated faces away from rough contact
* protect edges during turning and staging

Common risk:

* a panel can be lightweight but still flexible over a long span

---

# Onsite Handling Precautions

Site teams should control:

* worker spacing
* lifting route
* temporary staging location
* surface contact
* weather exposure
* edge protection
* tool contact

Do not:

* drag panels on the ground
* stack panels on uneven surfaces
* allow long panels to sag between supports
* place heavy objects on visible faces or interlocking edges
* leave panels exposed to rain or standing water

---

# Common Site Handling Risks

## Deformation From Improper Lifting

Risk:

* long panels may sag when lifted only from the ends

Control:

* use center support and coordinated multi-worker lifting

## Surface Scratches

Risk:

* tools, rough surfaces, and dragging can damage the coating

Control:

* protect coated faces and keep tools away from panel surfaces

## Edge Impact

Risk:

* corners and interlocking edges can deform during movement

Control:

* protect ends and avoid collision during turning, loading, and unloading

## Rain Exposure

Risk:

* exposed interiors, cuts, or stored panels can contact water

Control:

* keep panels covered, elevated, and dry before installation
