# Risk Visualization System

Risk visualization turns engineering risks into visible inspection cues in Technical Center pages.

The goal is not decoration. Visual marks should help readers understand where risk concentrates and what must be checked.

## Visual Risk Types

### Warning Zones

Use warning zones to mark areas where failure is likely if sequencing is wrong.

Examples:

- Opening head.
- Sill pocket.
- Lower drainage exit.
- Corner edge.
- Screw zone.
- Cut edge.

### Moisture Traps

Use moisture trap markers where water can enter but cannot leave.

Examples:

- Blocked sill outlet.
- Over-sealed base trim.
- Reverse-lap overlap.
- Closed cavity without drainage.

### Airflow Interruption

Use airflow indicators only where the assembly is designed to ventilate or dry.

Examples:

- Cavity drying path.
- Ventilation interruption near trim.
- Blocked breathing path.

### Thermal Leakage Areas

Use thermal risk zones where insulation continuity may be interrupted.

Examples:

- Fastener bridge.
- Trim bridge.
- Cut-edge gap.
- Opening transition.
- Panel joint.

### Unsupported Spans

Use support-risk markers where panel edges, pallet supports, or substrate conditions may permit deformation.

Examples:

- Unsupported corner edge.
- Weak substrate line.
- Long panel storage span.
- Base without continuous support.

## Annotation Rules

- Label the physical control point, not the marketing feature.
- Use arrows for movement: water, air, installation direction.
- Use warning marks for failure risk.
- Use zone lines for inspection bands.
- Pair each annotation with an inspection or correction note.

## Evidence Approval

An annotated image is useful only when it helps answer at least one question:

- What is the control point?
- What can fail?
- Where is the risk zone?
- What must be inspected?
- What evidence proves the detail is acceptable?
