# Fire Performance Logic

This document defines cautious fire-performance interpretation for Struvia exterior systems.

Reference base:

* uploaded technical specification Excel file
* `knowledge/product-structure.md`
* `knowledge/performance-system.md`

Important interpretation:

* Fire performance must be handled conservatively.
* Do not treat material heat resistance as fire resistance.
* Do not claim code compliance, non-combustibility, fire-rated wall assembly performance, or certified fire resistance without formal test documents.

---

# Spreadsheet Fire References

Extracted references:

* Chinese sheet fire rating: B2 level
* English sheet fire rating: General/B1
* heat resistance: 315 deg C
* structure: steel sheet, aluminum foil backing or coated fabric, and polyurethane foam composite

Data caution:

* the bilingual fire rating entries differ and must be verified before formal use
* fire rating terminology depends on test standard, sample type, and jurisdiction
* heat resistance describes behavior under elevated temperature exposure, not a full facade fire classification

---

# Fire Rating Interpretation

Fire rating should be interpreted only within its tested context.

Required context includes:

* test standard
* sample thickness and construction
* exposed face
* edge condition
* joint condition
* substrate or backing assembly
* pass criteria
* classification language

Without this context:

* B1 or B2 language should be treated as a reference label requiring confirmation
* it should not be converted into local code language
* it should not be used to claim full wall fire resistance

---

# Material Behavior Under Heat

Exterior composite behavior under heat depends on multiple layers.

Steel surface layer:

* provides a non-foam exterior facing
* may help protect the core from direct exposure in normal service conditions
* can transfer heat quickly because metal is conductive

PU foam core:

* contributes insulation and lightweight volume
* must be evaluated according to relevant fire test data
* should not be described as non-combustible without verified evidence

Backing layer:

* protects the rear side of the core
* may influence heat, smoke, and vapor behavior depending on assembly
* does not replace fire-stopping or code-required fire detailing

# Facade System Considerations

Fire performance is affected by the complete facade.

Review points:

* panel joints
* open cavities
* ventilation gaps
* openings
* corners
* penetrations
* fire-stopping requirements
* substrate combustibility
* building height and occupancy
* local exterior wall code requirements

Engineering caution:

* ventilated cavities can support drying, but may require fire-blocking or cavity barriers depending on code
* trims and panels should not interrupt required fire-stopping details
* fire strategy must be coordinated with architectural and engineering drawings
