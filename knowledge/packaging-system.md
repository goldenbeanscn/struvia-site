# Packaging Philosophy

Packaging is part of the exterior system workflow because it protects the geometry needed for installation.

Packaging must protect:

* surface coating
* panel edges
* interlocking profiles
* cut ends
* panel flatness
* package identification
* moisture-sensitive handling conditions

Engineering goal:

* reduce transport damage
* reduce surface abrasion
* reduce edge deformation
* keep packages stable during loading and unloading
* make export handling repeatable

---

# Carton Protection

The reference article describes carton packaging before palletizing or loading.

Article-derived logic:

* panels are grouped into cartons
* 8 or 10 panels per package are referenced
* packaging quantity depends on order and packing method
* labels are applied after boxing

Engineering role:

* cartons reduce direct surface contact
* cartons help separate panel groups
* cartons support package identification
* cartons reduce small scratches from handling

QC check:

* confirm correct panel count
* confirm finish and order grouping
* confirm carton closure
* confirm label placement and readability

---

# Pallet Sequencing

The article identifies pallet use for 2.9 m panel packaging.

Pallet logic:

* create a stable base for forklift handling
* reduce direct ground contact
* improve container loading sequence
* support repeatable package grouping

Engineering considerations:

* pallet dimensions must match package length and support points
* unstable pallets can create panel bending or package shift
* pallet sequence should consider unloading at destination

---

# Edge Reinforcement

Panel edges and corners are vulnerable during transport.

Article-derived logic:

* wooden planks are used in the packaging/loading workflow as reinforcement.

Engineering role:

* reduce edge impact risk
* reduce pressure concentration
* support long-panel packages
* protect package shape during loading

Control points:

* reinforce vulnerable ends
* prevent direct compression on interlocking edges
* avoid fasteners or rough reinforcement surfaces contacting visible panel faces

---

# Moisture Protection

Film sealing is part of the packaging workflow shown in the reference article.

Moisture-control logic:

* film wrap reduces direct rain or splash exposure during handling
* cartons and film should be kept intact before loading
* container interior should be checked for water, dirt, or residue

Important limit:

* packaging film is transport protection, not a substitute for dry storage or proper container condition.

Inspection points:

* torn film
* open carton edges
* wet packages
* dirty container floor
* trapped debris that may abrade packages

---

# Transport Stability

Transport stability prevents package movement and panel deformation.

Stability methods:

* grouped cartons
* pallet base where applicable
* film sealing
* plank reinforcement
* controlled loading order
* separation between package groups where needed

Risks:

* shifting during transport
* carton collapse
* surface abrasion
* edge crushing
* long-panel bending

---

# Friction Prevention

Friction prevention protects coated surfaces and carton integrity.

Control logic:

* avoid dragging cartons
* avoid loose debris between packages
* keep package faces aligned
* separate hard contact points
* prevent rough reinforcement materials from rubbing panel faces

Why it matters:

* transport vibration can turn small contact points into surface abrasion
* friction damage may not be visible until unloading

---

# Packaging Sequence

Recommended workflow based on article logic:

1. confirm panel order, finish, size, and quantity
2. group panels into cartons
3. apply package labels
4. place cartons onto pallets where applicable
5. apply film sealing
6. add plank or edge reinforcement where needed
7. inspect package stability
8. prepare packages for container loading

---

# Packaging QC Checklist

Check before loading:

* correct package count
* correct panel quantity per carton
* readable labels
* stable carton closure
* intact film wrapping
* edge reinforcement in place where required
* no visible moisture damage
* no crushed carton corners
* no exposed vulnerable panel edges
